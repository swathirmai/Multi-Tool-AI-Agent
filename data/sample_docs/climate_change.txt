Climate Change: Science and Impact
====================================

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns.
Since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change,
primarily due to burning fossil fuels.

Greenhouse Gases
-----------------
The primary greenhouse gases responsible for warming include:
- Carbon dioxide (CO2): Released by burning fossil fuels, deforestation
- Methane (CH4): Produced by livestock, landfills, natural gas leaks
- Nitrous oxide (N2O): From agriculture and industrial processes
- Water vapor: Amplifies warming caused by other gases

Current Temperature Trends
----------------------------
- Global average temperature has risen approximately 1.1°C since pre-industrial times
- The last decade (2011-2020) was the warmest on record
- Arctic regions are warming 2-4 times faster than the global average
- Sea levels rose about 20 cm over the 20th century

Key Impacts
------------
1. Extreme Weather: More frequent and intense heat waves, hurricanes, droughts
2. Sea Level Rise: Threatens coastal cities and island nations
3. Ecosystem Disruption: Coral bleaching, species migration, habitat loss
4. Food Security: Shifting agricultural zones, reduced crop yields
5. Human Health: Heat-related illness, spread of vector-borne diseases

Mitigation Strategies
----------------------
- Transition to renewable energy (solar, wind, hydropower)
- Energy efficiency improvements in buildings and transport
- Reforestation and forest conservation
- Carbon capture and storage technologies
- Sustainable agriculture practices

International Agreements
--------------------------
The Paris Agreement (2015) aims to limit global warming to 1.5-2°C above
pre-industrial levels. Countries submit Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs)
outlining their climate action plans.

The IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) publishes regular assessment
reports synthesizing the latest climate science.
